Okoy

What is Okoy?

Okoy is a traditional snack or appetizer with small shrimp and julienned vegetables, like sweet potatoes, carrots, and bean sprouts. These crispy fritters are bound together by a batter made with rice flour or cornstarch, then deep-fried until crispy and golden-brown.

Ingredients

  • 1 squash
  • 1 carrot
  • 1 large onion
  • 250 grams of tiny shrimp
  • a bit of cilantro
  • 2 teaspoons of baking powder
  • 1 cup of rice flour
  • 1 tablespoon of fish sauce
  • ¾ cup of water

Procedure

  1. Rinse the squash and remove any bumps on the skin. Cut a third or half, about a pound.
  2. Remove the seeds with a spoon.
  3. Cut it into thin strips using a julienne slicer, a julienne peeler, or a knife.
  4. Cut a medium carrot the same way.
  5. Thinly slice the onion.
  6. Roughly chop the cilantro stems and leaves.
  7. Combine squash, carrots, onion, and cilantro in a large bowl. Add salt and pepper to taste. Toss them together.
  8. For tiny shrimps, rinse them multiple times and remove any foreign bodies. They are sharp on both ends, so be careful when cleaning them.
  9. Season the shrimp with salt and pepper to taste.
  10. Make the batter by combining 1 cup of rice flour, 2 teaspoons of baking powder, 1 tablespoon of fish sauce, and ¾ cup of water. It will be thin and smooth, like a slurry.
  11. Pour it over the vegetables and toss them together.
  12. Set a skillet with an inch of oil over high heat. Dip a wooden spoon handle or a chopstick; it should steadily bubble when it's hot and ready.
  13. Spread about ½ cup of the mixture on a large spoon or turner, then slide it into the hot oil. Fry each side for about 2 minutes until golden brown. Drain them on paper towels.

Serve your Ukoy with a spicy vinegar dipping sauce and enjoy!
